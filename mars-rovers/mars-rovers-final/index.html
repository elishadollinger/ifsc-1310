Welcome to the Red Planet: Exploring Mars with NASA’s Rovers

For decades, NASA has been exploring the mysteries of Mars, sending robotic rovers across its rocky terrain in search of answers to some of humanity’s biggest questions: Is there life beyond Earth? What can Mars teach us about our own planet?

From the groundbreaking Pathfinder mission in 1997 to the cutting-edge Perseverance rover currently traversing Jezero Crater, these robotic explorers have revolutionized our understanding of the Red Planet. Each mission has built upon the last—testing new technologies, uncovering signs of ancient water, and paving the way for future human exploration.

This site is your launchpad into the world of NASA’s Mars rovers. Learn about the design and goals of each mission, discover the incredible science they’ve uncovered, and find out how these trailblazing robots are helping write the next chapter in space exploration.

Rover List

Opportunity

  • Mission overview: To study the Martian surface and geology to better understand the history of water on Mars.
  • Launch Date: June 10, 2003
  • Landing Date: January 4, 2004
  • Landing Site: Gusev Crater
  • Used airbag-assisted landing system
  • Major accomplishments
    • Found signs of past volcanic activity and hydrothermal systems
    • Discovered sulfate-rich soils indicating the presence of water
    • Operated over 6 years — far beyond its 90-day mission
  • Key design/technical features
    • Solar-powered with six wheels for rough terrain
    • Panoramic cameras and microscopic imagers
    • Robotic arm with rock-grinding tools
    • Experienced memory loss issues but still performed science ops

Curiosity

  • Mission goals and overview: To determine if Mars ever had the right environmental conditions to support microbial life.
  • Launch Date: November 26, 2011
  • Landing Date: August 6, 2012
  • Launch Vehicle: Atlas V 541 rocket
  • Landing Site: Gale Crater using the innovative sky crane system
  • Major accomplishments
    • Found evidence of ancient freshwater lakes
    • Detected complex organic molecules in Martian soil
    • Measured seasonal methane variations in the atmosphere
    • Identified clay-bearing rocks indicating past water activity
  • Key design/technical features
    • Powered by a radioisotope thermoelectric generator (RTG)
    • Equipped with a laser-firing instrument ("ChemCam") for rock analysis
    • Weighs ~2,000 pounds; about the size of a small SUV
    • Contains a weather station and radiation detectors

Background

The NASA Mars rover program is a pioneering effort to explore the Red Planet using robotic vehicles designed to traverse its surface and conduct scientific experiments. Beginning with Sojourner in 1997, the program has since launched a series of increasingly advanced rovers, including Spirit, Opportunity, Curiosity, and Perseverance. Each mission has contributed vital data about Mars’ geology, climate, and potential for past or present life.
