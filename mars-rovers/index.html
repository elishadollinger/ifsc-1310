Welcome to the Red Planet: Exploring Mars with NASA’s Rovers

For decades, NASA has been exploring the mysteries of Mars, sending robotic rovers across its rocky terrain in search of answers to some of humanity’s biggest questions: Is there life beyond Earth? What can Mars teach us about our own planet?

From the groundbreaking Pathfinder mission in 1997 to the cutting-edge Perseverance rover currently traversing Jezero Crater, these robotic explorers have revolutionized our understanding of the Red Planet.

This site is your launchpad into the world of NASA’s Mars rovers. Learn about the design and goals of each mission, discover the incredible science they’ve uncovered, and find out how these trailblazing robots are helping write the next chapter in space exploration.

Rover List

Opportunity

  • Mission overview: To analyze the Martian surface and look for past water activity.
  • Launch Date: July 7, 2003
  • Landing Date: January 25, 2004
  • Landing Site: Meridiani Planum (airbag-assisted)
  • Major accomplishments:
    • Discovered hematite blueberries
    • Proved wet, habitable conditions once existed
    • Traveled over 28 miles
  • Key design/technical features:
    • Solar-powered twin of Spirit
    • Rock abrasion tools and spectrometers
    • Autonomous driving capabilities

Spirit

  • Mission overview: To study the geology of Mars and search for water-related history.
  • Launch Date: June 10, 2003
  • Landing Date: January 4, 2004
  • Landing Site: Gusev Crater
  • Major accomplishments:
    • Found evidence of volcanic and hydrothermal activity
    • Identified sulfate-rich soils
    • Operated for over 6 years (planned for 90 days)
  • Key design/technical features:
    • Solar panels and six-wheel drive
    • Panoramic and microscopic imaging tools
    • Robotic arm with rock grinder

Curiosity

  • Mission goals and overview: To assess past habitability and study climate and geology.
  • Launch Date: November 26, 2011
  • Landing Date: August 6, 2012
  • Landing Site: Gale Crater (via sky crane system)
  • Major accomplishments:
    • Found clay-bearing rocks indicating water
    • Detected methane and organic molecules
    • Captured high-res terrain data
  • Key design/technical features:
    • Powered by a radioisotope thermoelectric generator (RTG)
    • Includes ChemCam laser analysis and weather station
    • Size of a small SUV with robust mobility

Background

The NASA Mars rover program is a pioneering effort to explore the Red Planet using robotic vehicles designed to traverse its surface and conduct scientific experiments. Beginning with Sojourner in 1997, the program has since launched a series of increasingly advanced rovers, including Spirit, Opportunity, Curiosity, and Perseverance.
